# iV7 Voter Accountability Act

## Purpose

This document defines a voter-accountability framework for iV7 governance and public-interest decision handling.

## Core Principles

- **Attribution:** Every proposal, vote, and amendment must preserve source attribution.
- **Traceability:** Each voting action must be timestamped and logged with an immutable record identifier.
- **Transparency:** Ballot language, eligibility rules, and results summaries must be available for review.
- **Integrity:** Duplicate, coerced, or proxy-manipulated votes are prohibited and subject to review.
- **Lawful Handling:** All accountability workflows must remain compliant with applicable law and tax requirements.

## Voting Record Requirements

For each voting cycle, maintain:

1. proposal identifier and full text,
2. eligible voter list and validation method,
3. vote cast record (anonymous/public mode specified),
4. tally method and verification notes,
5. final decision log with effective date,
6. chain-of-custody metadata for transferred records.

## Incident and Dispute Process

- Flag anomalous ballots, eligibility disputes, and tally mismatches as incidents.
- Preserve the complete evidence trail for each dispute.
- Require an independent review step before final incident closure.

## Enforcement

Repeated non-compliance with accountability controls may result in suspension of voting privileges until remediation is complete.
