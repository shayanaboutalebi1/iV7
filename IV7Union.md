# IV7 State, iV7 Union, and IV7 Voter Accountability Act

## Purpose
This document defines a neutral, civic-focused framework for three related working concepts:
- **IV7 State** (institutional governance model)
- **iV7 Union** (membership and coordination model)
- **IV7 Voter Accountability Act** (process accountability model)

## IV7 State (Concept Definition)
IV7 State is a draft governance concept intended to describe how a civic body could structure responsibilities and decision authority.

Core elements:
1. **Constitutional Basis:** Publish a charter that defines powers, limits, and rights protections.
2. **Separation of Roles:** Distinguish policy, operations, oversight, and adjudication functions.
3. **Public Recordkeeping:** Maintain traceable records for directives, votes, and revisions.
4. **Rights & Remedies:** Provide transparent complaint, review, and correction pathways.
5. **Lawful Operation:** Ensure all actions align with applicable local, state, and federal law.

## iV7 Union (Working Definition)
The iV7 Union is a voluntary coordination model intended to:
- Encourage lawful civic participation.
- Improve transparency between organizers and community members.
- Support non-violent, respectful dialogue across differing viewpoints.
- Maintain documented decision-making and role accountability.

## IV7 Voter Accountability Act (Concept Draft)
The IV7 Voter Accountability Act is framed as a policy concept with the following principles:
1. **Transparency:** Publish clear eligibility, procedures, and timelines for participation.
2. **Verification:** Use lawful, privacy-aware validation methods for records and reporting.
3. **Auditability:** Keep versioned logs for process decisions and vote-related actions.
4. **Accessibility:** Ensure participation pathways are understandable and broadly accessible.
5. **Non-Discrimination:** Apply rules consistently without targeting protected groups.
6. **Appeals:** Provide a documented mechanism to challenge procedural errors.
